About us

The Green Nigeria Movement (GNM) is established to encourage active youth participation in politics. The GNM is an initiative of the Nigerian Young Professionals Forum (NYPF) that seeks to enlighten the electorates on the need to participate actively in the forthcoming elections. GNM seeks to mobilize over 12 Million youth vote at all levels to vote in the 2023 Nigerian Presidential and Gubernatorial Elections.

GNM Objectives:

1. Improve the capacity of young professionals, including women and girls across the 36 states and 774 LGAs to coordinate and lead the campaign in their respective localities.
2. Increase citizens' access to electoral information through the creation of the Green Nigerian Movement platform for real time citizens awareness on the electoral process, political candidates profiling and electronic Collection of PVCs.
3. Increase voter turn-out through effective mobilization and partnership with key stakeholders including but not limited to civil society organizations, right holders ethnic and religious groups, community governance structures and the media.

GNM Target Population, Beneficiaries and Stakeholders.

● Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC)
● Market Women Association
● Student Union
● National Youth Service Corp (NYSC)
● Civil Society organizations
● Community Based Organizations
● Faith Based Organizations
● Disability Right Groups

The People's power is the most important resource in achieving a systemic democracy which is generally accepted to be the government of the people, for the people and by the people. Therefore, citizens' participation in democratic governance and decision-making at all levels is key towards achieving the sustainable development goals including a free and fair electoral process. Sadly, this is far-fetched in Nigeria, the most populous black Nation with an estimated 200 million people. Nigeria’s elections from 1999 up until the last Presidential elections in 2019 have been marked by voter apathy. In the 2015 Presidential election, out of 67,4 million registered voters only 29,4 million voted and in 2019 elections out of the 82.3 million registered voters as collated by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC), only 28.6 million voted reflecting that a small percentage between 18-30 percent of the voting population actually vote during elections outrightly contradicting the idea of ‘majority rule’ for the continent's most populous country. Citizens’ participation in the electoral process is necessary for democratic sustenance. Most electorates are unwilling to participate in elections due to diverse contextual factors ranging from lack of trust in the electoral system, insecurity at the polls and the credibility of aspirants. These and other factors place a huge demand to motivate, educate and engage citizens to actively participate and cast their votes during elections. To counter and change this narrative, The Nigerian Young Professionals Forum (NYPF), a Registered Non Governmental Organization, has launched the Green Nigeria Movement (GNM) to encourage active participation in the political process. The GNM is an initiative of the NYPF that seeks to enlighten the electorates on the need to participate actively in the forthcoming elections.
